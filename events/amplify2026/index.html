Undergrads, it's that time again!

We are back for the 3rd Edition of our undergraduate physics symposium for Black and mixed-Black students in the UK.

After two successful editions, this year, we are bringing the same energy and more.. With an incredible line-up of Black physicists at every stage of their journey, this is your space to connect, expand your network, learn and just vibe!

OUR MISSION

We know that doing a physics degree can be challenging, and can sometimes feel harder with the lack of representation, so we have created a space for undergraduates to build connections, explore shared experiences as Black physicists and feel empowered for your next steps.

WHAT, WHERE & WHEN?

Amplify 2026 will be a two day residential event hosted by us at The University of Wawrick on Thursday 10th to Friday 11th September 2026.

Day one begins in the afternoon:

  • You'll get to meet the other students and put your head together on a group task.
  • You'll hear from people who are doing or have done a PhD, from their experiences to their cutting-edge research.
  • There will be an evening reception for you to relax and make new connections.

Day two features:

  • An inspiring keynote lecture delivered by a leading Black physicist
  • A blend of interactive sessions about career options, alternative paths and much more.

And of course, we’ll have some tasty Afro-Caribbean food!

Why come along?

🤝🏽 A chance to LINK UP!

This is a great opportunity to meet other Black undergraduates, PhD students and graduates with careers both within and outside of academia. The event serves an amazing opportunity to be inspired by leading physicists across the UK. Be prepared to discover what your degree can offer you and the doors it can open for you.

💭 Unsure about your next steps in physics?

The career options for physicists are almost infinite. Whether you’re thinking of PhDs, industry or finance, come along to consider what you need to do for your next step and hear about other Black physicists’ journeys into the working world.

🎯 Finding your place as a physicist

This is the perfect event for you to reflect on your unique perspective. What better place to do this than amongst other bright Black physicists? This is the place where you definitely won’t be the odd one out.

TRAVEL GRANTS & ACOMMODATION

There will be travel bursaries available, so that you don't have to worry about the cost of getting to The University of Warwick in Coventry. One-nights accommodation? That will be covered by us too!

Am I eligible?

We recognise the fact that people of Black-heritage are severely under-represented amongst those studying and working in physics and physics-related fields. Therefore, we encourage you to apply if you are:

  • An undergraduate physics student in the UK of Black or mixed-Black heritage
  • Going into 2nd, 3rd, 4th or 5th (Scotland) year of a physics degree at a UK university
  • Or graduated with a physics degree in 2025 or 2026
