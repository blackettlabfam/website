Undergrads, this one's for you.

We are thrilled to announce the first-ever symposium for Black and mixed-Black undergraduate physics students in the UK!

An important mission for us is to connect and support Black physicists across the UK, and we thought it’s time to bring the undergraduates together to link up! We know that doing a physics degree can be tough, so we wanted to create space for undergrads to build connections, explore what it means to be Black in physics and feel empowered to take their next steps.

WHAT, WHERE & WHEN?

Amplify 2024 will be a one-day, in-person event hosted by us at The University of Manchester on Thursday 12th September 2024.

The day will be a fantastic blend of interactive sessions to chat about the latest physics research and career discussions to discover the many paths open to students after post graduation. There will be plenty of space and time for students to reflect on themselves as awesome Black physicists in the UK, and of course we’ll make sure to provide only the best food.

What can I expect?

Connect with other Black physicists..

This will be a invaluable opportunity to meet other Black undergrad students, graduates, PhD students and academics with careers both within and outside of academia from across the UK. We have prepared a mash-up of interactive sessions, talks on the latest research and panel discussions to discover the many paths open to students after post-graduation.

Consider your next steps..

The career options for physicists are *almost* infinite. Whether you’re thinking of PhDs, industry or finance, come along to consider what you need to do for your next step and hear about other Black physicists’ journeys into the working world.

Take up space!

This event is an opportunity for you to reflect on your unique perspective as a Black physicist in the UK, and what better place to do this than amongst other bright Black physicists. There will be plenty of space and time for students to connect, reflect on their journeys as a physicists, and of course we’ll make sure to that the food is top-tier.

Am I eligible?

We recognise the fact that people of Black-heritage are severely underrepresented among those studying physics and physics-related subjects, and working in physics-related fields. Our mission is to change that! Therefore, we encourage you to apply if you are:

  • An undergraduate physics student in the UK of Black or mixed-Black heritage
  • Going into 2nd, 3rd, 4th or 5th (Scotland) year of a physics degree at a UK university

TRAVEL COSTS?

There will be travel bursaries available, so that you don’t have to worry about the cost of getting to Manchester.
More details can be found on the registration form.
